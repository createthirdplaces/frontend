Bars, Malls, and Restaurants as Third Places

{{>authors/articleHeading name='Bharat Ponnaluri' date='May 2026' imageLink='https://createthirdplaces.nyc3.cdn.digitaloceanspaces.com/people/Bharat_Picture.jpg' }}

Bars, malls, and resturants are widely used as public spaces. However, they aren't always third places designed for people to linger and interact. Here are criteria that should be considered when evaluating a bar, mall, or restaurant as a third place.

  • Ability of local residents to walk there.
    • online ordering.
  • Space availaiblity including table space and opening hours.
  • Ability to stay for a long period of time.
  • Ability for people to regularly use the space. This includes affordable food prices, acessibility, and being welcoming to anyone who wants to use the space.
  • Support for interaction with other people. This includes humans taking food orders instead of kiosks or phone ordering, and the ability to easily have conversations with other patrons.

An ideal third place

One example of a third place was the Landing in Arlington, Virginia, which is shown in the image at the top of this page. It was open 24/7 with plenty of available table space, and people could stay there as long as they wanted. There was a wide variety of low cost food options nearby, and people could also chose to not purchase anything. Also, people who lived nearby could easily walk there.

An example of a bar that was missing some of the qualities of a third place

The Board Room in Arlington, Virgina was a popular public gathering space until it closed in April 2026.

Reasons the Board Room was a good third place

  • Walkability: It was easy for residents living at nearby homes to walk nearby. There were also apartments built on floors above the Board Room.
  • Food was ordered by speaking to a bartener or server.
  • When space was available, people could stay until the Board Room closed. In 2026 before it closed, the Board Room was open until at least midnight every day.

Reasons the Board Room was not a good third place

  • Food prices: Food at the Board Room became very expensive. In April 2026, a typical meal cost well over $10, with a limited selection of cheaper options.
  • Inconsistent table space availability: Sometimes, table space was not available due to crowds or reservations for events. There was also table space in side rooms that people could not use when I went there on Wednesday evenings.
  • It was someones difficult to have conversations due to loud music or background noise from activities the Board Room was running.
