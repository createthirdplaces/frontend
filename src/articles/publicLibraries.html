The value of public libraries as community space in DC

{{>authors/articleHeading name='Bharat Ponnaluri' date='April 2026' imageLink='https://createthirdplaces.nyc3.cdn.digitaloceanspaces.com/people/Bharat_Picture.jpg' }} Mount Pleasant Branch Library. (By JuliaC2006 from Rochester, UK - Mount Pleasant library, CC BY 2.0, Link)

I am a resident of DC living in Dupont Circle who hosts community board game events and advocates for more public space. DC Public Library(DCPL) has done a great job of running libraries and making them public spaces for the benefit of the community. It is critical for DCPL to have enough funding to continue their success.

Finding space to host events in DC has become increasingly difficult and other hosts I've talked to have had challenges. I was part of one group that moved it's regular location 4 times within the span of a year.

Over the past 10 years, I have noticed a significant decline in the availability of accessible community gathering spaces in DC. Back in 2016, many of the community gathering spaces were in bars and restaurants that are no longer options. Many places have closed, shortened their hours, or raised food prices. While many new restaurants have opened, the ones with space typically have high food prices.

Former site of a Noodles & Company restaurant near Farragut square that closed in 2020. It is still vacant. (Photo by Bharat Ponnaluri)

Many locations such as the Panera on the south side of DuPont Circle are now empty commercial lots. With the decreasing public space availability, libraries in DC are becoming increasingly important as public spaces. They are free to use, and provide space for community groups. They are also great spaces to learn through community classes and reading.

Picture of the south DuPont Circle Metro. The vacant former Panera location is on the right. (Photo by Bharat Ponnaluri)

Community spaces such as libraries in DC are great for these types of events because they can be used for free. However, hosting events in libraries isn't always a feasible option due to limitations on hours or space availability. This is a sign that library space should be increases to meet interest.

Why libraries are great

As accessible community spaces, libraries have the following benefits.

  • Incremental neighborhood improvements are best done by residents working together. More community spaces would give more opportunities for neighbors to connect with each other, and form friendships that will translate into working on neighborhood improvements.
  • Public health would improve as the community spaces include sports facilities where people can exercise. Expanded access to local recreational spaces would make people more connected with their communities and neighbors. In DC, travelling to a local space would likely involve walking and possibly public transit. This allows them to get a better view of their community and connect with other locals. At these community spaces, people would form in person connections with their community.

Recommendations

Here are the areas where I think DCPL has been the most successful. Continued support is essential to make sure DCPL can provide these important activities.

1. Support community groups using libraries

2. Support adult learning services

3. Avoid staff cuts

5. Use libraries as a way to demonstrate how what great community spaces look like
