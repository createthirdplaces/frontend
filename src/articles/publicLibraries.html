The value of public libraries

{{>authors/bharatPonnaluriHeading}}

I am resident of DC living in Dupont Circle who hosts community board game events and advocates for more public space. Finding space to host events in DC has become increasingly difficult and other hosts I've talked to have had challenges. I was part of one group that moved it's regular location 4 times within the span of a year.

Over the past 10 years, I have noticed a significant decline in the availability of accessible community gathering spaces in DC. Back in 2016, many of the community gathering spaces were in bars and restaurants that are no longer options. Many places have closed, shortened their hours, or raised food prices. While many new restaurants have opened, the ones with space typically have high food prices.

Many locations such as the Panera on the souther side of DuPont Circle are now empty commercial lots. With the decreasing public space availability, libraries in DC are becoming increasingly important as public spaces. They are free to use, and provide space for community groups. They are also great spaces to learn through community classes and reading.

Community spaces such as libraries in DC are great for these types of events because they can be used for free. However, hosting events in libraries isn't always a feasible option due to limitations on hours or space availability. This is a sign that library space should be increases to meet interest

Why libraries are great

As accessible community spaces, libraries have the following benefits.

  • Incremental neighborhood improvements are best done by residents working together. More community spaces would give more opportunities for neighbors to connect with each other, and form friendships that will translate into working on neighborhood improvements.
  • Public health would improve as the community spaces include sports facilities where people can exercise. Expanded access to local recreational spaces would make people more connected with their communities and neighbors. In DC, travelling to a local space would likely involve walking and possibly public transit. This allows them to get a better view of their community and connect with other locals. At these community spaces, people would form in person connections with their community.

Recommendations

Here are some things DC public libraries are doing that they should prioritize more

1. Support community groups using library

2. Support adult learning services

3. Avoid staff cuts

4. Maintain library hours and focus on expanding them in the long term.

5. Use libraries as a way to demonstrate how what great community spaces look like
