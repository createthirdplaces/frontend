Local advocacy and third places

By Bharat Ponnaluri

Introduction

Local advocacy is an important part of a thriving community, and third places are a key part of supporting them. This article will explain the benefits of local advocacy, analyze various locations to show the positive effects of local advocacy. The second part will discuss how third places support local advocacy and urban planning policies that support third places. The third part will contain information about taking action.

Ancient Rome and other historical examples

One reason the Roman Empire was successful is the fact that relative to its neighbors, it was good at being inclusive to its people and conquests. The Roman Empire was a highly unequal society built on exploitation of human labor and plunder. However, the tradition of civic engagment for a long time that reduced the impact of these problems.

Later, the Roman Empire had issues where wealth started being consolidated in the hands of rich people, while slavery continued. Attempts to earn money thorugh plunder also continued. People started caring less about Rome’s success because they didn’t see much to gain from Rome’s rule.

During the 5th century AD, the Western Roman Empire fell. As the Western Roman Empire lost territory, former Romans living in those territories often did not care about trying to restore Roman rule.

A similiar pattern was seen with the Byzantine Empire which lost control over most of Anatolia in the 11th and 12th centuries. Turks took over and many residents did not care or welcomed Turkish rule. During this time, Byanztine leaders were busy trying to prioritize personal power over supporting an inclusive civil society that people wanted to be a part of.

During this time, the Byanzine Empire allow a massacre of Latin residents from Italy to take place, which ended up contributing to the long term instability and fall of Constantionople.

Ancient Sparta was another example where the lack of inclusive civic engagement led to it's downfall. Sparta was the dominant power for some time in Ancient Greece, but it was run by a small elite while most of the population known as helots lived as slaves.

Relations with other cultures.

When Rome made some efforts to encourage good relations with other culture, Rome was able to get along with them and treat them with relative decency. On the other hand, Rome's mistreatment of places like Carthage and Corinth, encouraged continued acts of unjustified violence against both cities. Eventually, the violence contributed to Rome deciding to burn both of those cities.

Successful countries across history are ones where people are invested in the success without being overly nationalistic. A country cannot be successful if nobody cares about helping it do well. On the other hand, excessive nationalism is also bad. It directs the country to be aggressive towards neighbors and target people living in the country for arbitrary reasons. It also makes people focus on arbitrary things that are seen as being in service of the nationalism. It doesn’t matter how good a country may be doing on paper in terms of measures such as wealth and territory, trying to harm others is not a measure of success. Rome is a partial historical example of this due to how Roman people’s attitudes towards helping had an effect. While the Romans were known for nationalism gone too far such as destroying Carthage when the city wasn’t threatening Rome, and mass enslavement, Roman history still shows how civic engagement had a positive effect in other areas. For example, Rome was often very successful, even after suffering from major defeats such as Cannae during the Second Punic War. The key was Romans cared about the success of their country. Apathy towards maintaining the Roman empire was a major factor in causing the empire to decline. Yes, there were a lot of external problems such as the Huns invading. However, these problems were exacerbated by ineffective Roman responses. Many Romans were preoccupied with other things or they were busy fighting each other in destructive civil wars.

Ancient farming and the relevance to today

Ancient farmers were focused on survival and minimizing the risk of inconsistent harvests. As a result, they did not prioritize efficiency. 1.

Here are some lessons learned that can be applied to modern-day organizations.

Tech startups and tech projects need to operate in a manner similiar to ancient farmers to manage costs. Like farmers, they need to prioritize short term survival, even if is more inefficient in the long turn.

Peace in an area during ancient times would lead to the development of a trade network that ancient farmers could use to mitigate risk. As a result, farmers could spend their money more efficiently. For example, money could be used to buy imported food, and this is something that would not be an option without a functioing trade network.

Minimizing costs is important. It reduces the need to make short term decisions with logn term costs. Lower costs also make it easier to build up a buffer to reduce the risk of short term losses.

Political infighting among ancient leaders meant they weren't as focused on supporting trade networks. People became more focused on gaining power for themselves and short term survival. The infighting also often escalted to civil wars that disrupted trade. In organizational politics, infighting is likely to cause signficiant issues for a project, especially if it escalates to people getting fired. This promotes an environment where peple are focused on keeping their jobs and organizational prestige.

The benefits of local advocacy

Local advocacy has significant benefits for neighborhoods in terms of quality of life and health. It also makes local economiess more sustainable. On the other hand, a lack of local advocacy can cause a neighborhood to be vulnerable to real estate bubbles that have long term effects.

Social cohesion

There are a lot of complex ongoing challenges we need to deal with such as climate change and poverty. Solving these challenges requires people to work together, and working together requires people meeting each other. I also believe that people will lead happier and healthier lives with more in person interaction. These interactions will give people changes to make more friends, and pursue their interests in a safe and healthy manner. Technological development and pro social values Pro social values become increasingly important as tech becomes more powerful. As technology gets more powerful, its ability to cause destruction increases. However, it’s ability to benefit society increases.Giving up technology would be irresponsible as it would be a way of making our lives worse for no reason. It is also impractical as we have become used to living with technology. As a result, the best way to deal with new technology is to promote more prosocial values. If we fail to develop more pro social values, the ability of technology to cause destruction will become an existential risk. I’m also concerned about tech reducing pro social values because it makes it harder f or people to deal with social issues. I think AI can be a threat to society, and it’s not because of super intelligent killer robots. AI can promote anti-social values by replacing human interaction such as with chatpots, so they are less concerned about being constructive with technology. It can also be used to cause people to develop a psychological dependence on technology, which prevents them from trying to deal with issues such as climate change.

Taking action

Using software as a tool

Software is helpful for advocacy. However, it should be used as a tool in specific areas where it makes sense. Software should not be seen as the default solution for a problem. This section will include a signficant amount of high level advice aside from specific areas. People work differently, and technology that is useful for one person may not be useful for other.

First of all, when using technology, you should be aware of why you are using it. Otherwise, technology can end up using a lot of your time. There have been many instances where I decide I'm going to quickly check something online, and end up browsing for more than an hour.

Internet usage can also have negative effects besides wasting time. Information overload from browsing a lot of content can be a distraction. There is also content that is misleading, incorrect, or designed to trigger negative emotions.

One useful rule of thumb is to make sure the app or website you are using has very clear boundaries on functionality, like looking at email, or going to a weather forecast website to see if will rain in the evening.

Also, be careful about chasing technology hype by focusing on new technology just because someone says it is good. It takes time for people to determine if a technology is useful and where it is worth using. Also, new technologies are likely to be in an early state of development and may change significantly in the upcoming future.

For example, there was a time when blockchain technology was seen as the next big thing. Cryptocurrencies and NFTs were viewed as the main applications of blockchains. After people learned more about blockchain and experimented with use cases, people lost interest in the technology and stopped seeing it as a game changer.

The hype behind NFTs was arguably worse than the hype around cryptocurrencies, as NFTs were even less useful. The idea behind an NFT was that it was a piece of digital art that used blockchain technlogy to track ownership. However, showing an NFT meant that someone could just take a screenshot and have a copy for themselves.

The risk of decreased efficiency with using AI

If a company has success with a tool, jobs will be cut, which can lead people to start useless projects that cause inefficiency. Also, the effectiveness of AI tools is inconsistent, and the productivity drain from tools being ineffective needs to be factored in. There will also be a wide disparity in the quality of AI tools and their adoption rates. As a result, some groups will see rapid short term productivity gains relative to others. They will become highly desirable, so getting in will become highly competitive. This will breed an overly competitive environment that causes long term problems. People will deliberately start useless projects or ones that make a product worse if it means boosting their reputation among managers. Often managers want useless products or features that make the user experience worse because of money or because the update is seen as a good thing by influential people. An efficient AI is also going to lead to job cuts, so people will come up with useless projects in order to justify their jobs. A team that is successful using AI to increase productivity will see a rapid spike in revenue and prestige, and there will be external and internal pressure to continue that prestige. However, the prestige gain might be based on a successful product or update that can’t be replicated with something else. As a result, they will start coming up with useless ideas that maintain the prestige and profit, even if they cause long term problems or degrade product quality. These useless projects will become entrenched and cause increasing amounts of inefficiency.

Prioritizing UX.

It is a well-known fact that UX should be prioritized. However, the reality is that UX is often ignored. It is important to understand why in order to address the issue.

I think the issue is related to misaligned incentives and a lack of understanding of issues. UX issues seem to be significantly affecting revenue, even when companies are profitable for other reasons. Often, I noticed a customer or internal stakeholder at work will ask for a new feature. Other people will get on board to seem lie they are supporting something useful. ON the other hand customers are far less likely to suggest UX improvements and might just chose to stop using a product with poor UX. An internal stakeholder is less likely to see UX issues as a problem, since they aren’t actively using the product to solve a problem. It is far harder to sell improved UX as something that useful because a new feature is more noticeable to upper management. As a result, stakeholders are incentivized to focus on ignoring UX in order to look good to upper management. Another issue is the proliferation of one size fits all software and tools such as Tailwind CSS and GitHub Copiilot that do not account for different UX preferences. ALsoo, the frameworks are not capable of talking to customers and finding out what users are looking for Different people have different perspectives on what good UX is. For example, Arabic is read from right to left, so someone who knows Arabic and not English is going to have a different idea of what they want a user interface to be compared to someone who can only read English.

Why language intelligibility explains challenges with tool churcn and increasing complexity

There are a variety of languages that are spoken around the world. People who speak two different languages can communicate by both learning a third common language, learning each others’ language, or communicating with the help of a translator. They work because of predictability. Once a person is fluent in a language, a person will typically not forget to speak a language. Languages also change slowly, which helps people maintain the ability to speak and understand the language. The consistent patterns make it easier to learn because learning materials are more consistent. Programming tools tend to change somewhat quickly relative to languages. Imagine if someone who was fluent in Spanish 10 years ago would not be fluent now, or they would be fluent in another language you didn't know. Things would devolve into chaos. Lately, organizations have been creating their own tools. They include concepts specific to the tool that do not apply to other tools. Examples include AWS, Tailwind CSS, and React. Because organizations are creating so many tools with their own specific guidelines for how things should be done, becoming an expert in everything is impossible. This is even true when focusing on tools in one area. For example, AWS, Google Cloud Platform and Azure are 3 major cloud providers and there are many smaller ones. Systems will become harder and harder to maintain as they are designed around increasingly complex stacks of products that most people do not know. This increasing complexity is a natural consequence of the fact that these systems are already complex. When a system is complex, people have a hard time figuring out the ideal way of making a change. As a result, they end up making a change in a way that adds complexity. Guidelines for different tools are not logically consistent with each other, which increases onboarding challenge, and makes increasing tech debt more likely. During 2025, tech companies saw record profits and stock prices. These are being driven by monopolies and clever marketing. As a result, they aren’t incentivized to spend money on building better products. Eventually, the complexity of their systems will become unmaintainable to the point where they can't be modified to manipulate users and generate ad revenue. As a result, profits will collapse and they will return to a more sustainable profit level or fail. As a result tech companies or the companies that replace them will have to focus on building better products. One example of a company that will have challenges is Amazon. They make a significant portion of their profits from AWS. As tech companies decline or go out of business, they will earn less. Also, their AWS infrastructure will become harder to maintain and people will move to competitors. Two other examples are Google and Meta, which make money from ads on websites that people could stop using.

Value of computer science as a humanities discipline for software development.

Right now, computer science is typically viewed as an engineering discipline, when I think it should be viewed as a humanities discipline. I see the most important parts of software development are understanding what users want, understanding the cultural norms of the potential users, and then creating something that has a positive impact. I see logic with writing code as a tool to achieve these goals. Writing logic for the sake of writing logic is not appealing to me. For the same reason, I’m not interested in talking about tech for the sake of talking about tech. I’m interested in talking about tech in the context of real world impact. This also means I have a hard time focusing on a new feature based on written or val instructions that don’t come directly from a user. This is true, even if I know something will have a positive impact.

How large projects trend towards having tech debt without consistency

I think software often ends up with issues because people are adding code that is organized in different ways. This is especially true for code that runs in a web browser. Someone new who is trying to understand how the code works to make changes often has a hard time because of the inconsistent code causing confusion. This leads to lower quality code that has bugs because a developer is less likely to make a correct change. I think most existing templates don’t do a great job of enforcing consistent code(React, Vue, etc) There are some technologies such as Angular and Next that do. However, they often require a custom programming language or configurations of specific dependencies. These tools also may not be used in the future, and often introduce patterns which are not relevant outside the tools. Learning how to build my own template means working with JavaScript and HTML, which are going to be used in the future, and they provide helpful knowledge when working with specific template tools in the future.

One way to mitigate this is to have strict boundaries between parts of the application. It is easier to maintain consistency for a small part of the application, If JavaScript is used on the server and browser, it is easier for bad practices to spread. At the same time, it is harder to enforce good practices because people see the need to enforce them on the UI and backend. People also misapply patterns from on location to the other Also, someone new who is trying to understand how the code works to make changes often has a hard time because of the inconsistent code causing confusion. With a codebase that has strict boundaries, it is easier to modify one part of the codebase to maintain one pattern while the others are understood to have another pattern.

Tech companies and trending towards inefficiency

Employees are often asked to work in ways that are inefficient due to corporate politics, or by managers not knowing that they are encouraging inefficiency. Employee behaviors compound this inefficiency. They will invent pointless work and unnecessary complexity to themselves seem as more productive to managers. They also enjoy complexity. Inconsistent levels of success on a day to day basis contribute to this, because there is a general expectation of consistency. There are also times where there is not enough work available to engineers, which contributes to the creation of unnecessary complexity, which causes inefficiency. Improvements in tech without changes in how we work will compound these problems. I see two main problems with these accumulated inefficiencies. First, these inefficiencies often cause issues related to customer health or well being, or make them have a bad experience. One example of these inefficiency is with AI customer service that doesn’t effectively solve customer issues. Safety concerns are often a result of these inefficiencies. These accumulated inefficiencies also lead to problems handling a crisis, as we saw when a CrowdStrike update led to millions of Windows computers being bricked, and CrowdStrike did not have a way of quickly dealing with the issue. This led to numerous flight cancellations among many other issues. Being efficient requires having developed habits of efficiency. An inefficient organization can’t magically turn into an efficient one.

Why I think AGI is not possible

The impossibility of developing and maintaining AGI is one reason why I’m not allowing the use of LLM generated code. Sustainable AGI is impossible, even with infinite computing power. Computers are designed in a fundamentally different way from people. Trying to achieve AGI is like trying to chase a dragon that keeps running away. This South Park video is an example: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FkJmgF6ptsk The main reason AGI is impossible is the fact that the human brain is a biological construct with biological input sources, which is complex. Organizational politics will prevent any organization from building a complex computerized system that recreates the biological nature of the human brain. The Roman Empire’s inability to conquer Germany shows that AGI is impossible. Rome failed to conquer Germany for political reasons. While Rome was theoretically capable of sending large numbers of legions to Germany and making sure they were supplied, Romans weren't willing to soy the cost. Due to the complexity of a theoretical AGI system, it would have to be created and maintained by a large group of people. Large groups of people aren’t a hive mind that is perfectly in sync. They have a variety of thinking patterns and goals, which will make an AGI organization impossible to maintain.

Communication challenges will also prevent AGI from being achieved. A successful AGI system is going to be complex and require large teams of people working together and sharing knowlege. People who don’t communicate will drift apart. There are plenty of historical examples where one group of people that are part of the same country ends up splitting up when that country loses territory. This leads to cultural divergence. We can also notice a similar process occurring over a shorter period of time. The size of ancient empires was limited by communication challenges, and a similar principle also applies to large organizations. Communication and transportation technology has started to plateau, which is a limiting feature for technology development. Like ancient empires, organizational political conflict and communication overhead will limit the ability of companies to improve technology and make AI smart

Using offline alternatives to tech

Often, it is helpful to use offline alternatives to tech, even if they mean taking additional time. Much of my research for this article came from physical books and newspapers. Conversations with other people were also very helpful for learning about issues related to civic advocacy.

For example, I went to the Charlie Clark Center for Local History at Arlington County's Center library to do research. I was looking for historical information about local advocacy in Arlington County.

During one visit there, I had a brief conversation with the manager about the research I was doing. I mentioned that I was looking for information about local advocay related to the planning of the DC Metro in the 1960s. Shortly after the conversation, he handed me a book called "The Great Society Subway by Zachary M. Schrag", which ended up being very helpful for my research due to the analysis of planning for the DC Metro.

Later, I ended up running into him during an event for the Arlington's Aurora Hills Library Centennial Celebration. We had a brief conversation about Arlington's support for school desegration, and he told me that Arlington was the second county in the nation to desegrate schools.

Looking through books and newspaper clippings took a significant amount of time. However, it was great for learning, and they weren't distracting me like the Internet would. There was also information that was not available online.

While offline activites are helpful, it is important to not completely avoid technology and ignore any instances where it is useful. I typed this article on a computer, and did not write it by hand on paper. Also, I looked through online newspaper archives to do research for this article.
