Local advocacy and third places

By Bharat Ponnaluri

Introduction

Local advocacy is an important part of a thriving community, and third places are a key part of supporting them. This article will explain the benefits of local advocacy, analyze various locations to show the positive effects of local advocacy. The second part will discuss how third places support local advocacy and urban planning policies that support third places. The third part will contain information about taking action.

The Roman Empire and other historical examples

While it has been a long time since the Roman Empire fell, there are lessons from the Roman empire that can be applied to local advocacy today.

The Roman Empire was a highly unequal society built on exploitation of human labor and plunder. However, it did have a tradition of civic engagmeent for a long time that reduces the impact of these problems. In the later stages of the Roman empire, a decrease in civic engagement led to more exploitation of human labor and plunder as people spent more time prioritizing enriching themselves.

Ancient Sparta was another example where the lack of inclusive civic engagement led to it's downfall. Sparta was the dominant power for some time in Ancient Greece, but it was run by a small elite while most of the population known as helots lived as slaves.

The benefits of local advocacy

Local advocacy has significant benefits for neighborhoods in terms of quality of life and health. It also makes local economiess more sustainable. On the other hand, a lack of local advocacy can cause a neighborhood to be vulnerable to real estate bubbles that have long term effects.

Social cohesion

There are a lot of complex ongoing challenges we need to deal with such as climate change and poverty. Solving these challenges requires people to work together, and working together requires people meeting each other. I also believe that people will lead happier and healthier lives with more in person interaction. These interactions will give people changes to make more friends, and pursue their interests in a safe and healthy manner. Technological development and pro social values Pro social values become increasingly important as tech becomes more powerful. As technology gets more powerful, its ability to cause destruction increases. However, it’s ability to benefit society increases.Giving up technology would be irresponsible as it would be a way of making our lives worse for no reason. It is also impractical as we have become used to living with technology. As a result, the best way to deal with new technology is to promote more prosocial values. If we fail to develop more pro social values, the ability of technology to cause destruction will become an existential risk. I’m also concerned about tech reducing pro social values because it makes it harder f or people to deal with social issues. I think AI can be a threat to society, and it’s not because of super intelligent killer robots. AI can promote anti-social values by replacing human interaction such as with chatpots, so they are less concerned about being constructive with technology. It can also be used to cause people to develop a psychological dependence on technology, which prevents them from trying to deal with issues such as climate change.

Taking action

Using software as a tool

Software is helpful for advocacy. However, it should be used as a tool in specific areas where it makes sense. Software should not be seen as the default solution for a problem. This section will include a signficant amount of high level advice aside from specific areas. People work differently, and technology that is useful for one person may not be useful for other.

First of all, when using technology, you should be aware of why you are using it. Otherwise, technology can end up using a lot of your time. There have been many instances where I decide I'm going to quickly check something online, and end up browsing for more than an hour.

Internet usage can also have negative effects besides wasting time. Information overload from browsing a lot of content can be a distraction. There is also content that is misleading, incorrect, or designed to trigger negative emotions.

One useful rule of thumb is to make sure the app or website you are using has very clear boundaries on functionality, like looking at email, or going to a weather forecast website to see if will rain in the evening.

Also, be careful about chasing technology hype by focusing on new technology just because someone says it is good. It takes time for people to determine if a technology is useful and where it is worth using. Also, new technologies are likely to be in an early state of development and may change significantly in the upcoming future.

For example, there was a time when blockchain technology was seen as the next big thing. Cryptocurrencies and NFTs were viewed as the main applications of blockchains. After people learned more about blockchain and experimented with use cases, people lost interest in the technology and stopped seeing it as a game changer.

The hype behind NFTs was arguably worse than the hype around cryptocurrencies, as NFTs were even less useful. The idea behind an NFT was that it was a piece of digital art that used blockchain technlogy to track ownership. However, showing an NFT meant that someone could just take a screenshot and have a copy for themselves.

The risk of decreased efficiency with using AI

If a company has success with a tool, jobs will be cut, which can lead people to start useless projects that cause inefficiency. Also, the effectiveness of AI tools is inconsistent, and the productivity drain from tools being ineffective needs to be factored in. There will also be a wide disparity in the quality of AI tools and their adoption rates. As a result, some groups will see rapid short term productivity gains relative to others. They will become highly desirable, so getting in will become highly competitive. This will breed an overly competitive environment that causes long term problems. People will deliberately start useless projects or ones that make a product worse if it means boosting their reputation among managers. Often managers want useless products or features that make the user experience worse because of money or because the update is seen as a good thing by influential people. An efficient AI is also going to lead to job cuts, so people will come up with useless projects in order to justify their jobs. A team that is successful using AI to increase productivity will see a rapid spike in revenue and prestige, and there will be external and internal pressure to continue that prestige. However, the prestige gain might be based on a successful product or update that can’t be replicated with something else. As a result, they will start coming up with useless ideas that maintain the prestige and profit, even if they cause long term problems or degrade product quality. These useless projects will become entrenched and cause increasing amounts of inefficiency.

Why I think AGI is not possible

The impossibility of developing and maintaining AGI is one reason why I’m not allowing the use of LLM generated code. Sustainable AGI is impossible, even with infinite computing power. Computers are designed in a fundamentally different way from people. Trying to achieve AGI is like trying to chase a dragon that keeps running away. This South Park video is an example: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FkJmgF6ptsk The main reason AGI is impossible is the fact that the human brain is a biological construct with biological input sources, which is complex. Organizational politics will prevent any organization from building a complex computerized system that recreates the biological nature of the human brain. The Roman Empire’s inability to conquer Germany shows that AGI is impossible. Rome failed to conquer Germany for political reasons. While Rome was theoretically capable of sending large numbers of legions to Germany and making sure they were supplied, Romans weren't willing to soy the cost. Due to the complexity of a theoretical AGI system, it would have to be created and maintained by a large group of people. Large groups of people aren’t a hive mind that is perfectly in sync. They have a variety of thinking patterns and goals, which will make an AGI organization impossible to maintain.

Communication challenges will also prevent AGI from being achieved. A successful AGI system is going to be complex and require large teams of people working together and sharing knowlege. People who don’t communicate will drift apart. There are plenty of historical examples where one group of people that are part of the same country ends up splitting up when that country loses territory. This leads to cultural divergence. We can also notice a similar process occurring over a shorter period of time. The size of ancient empires was limited by communication challenges, and a similar principle also applies to large organizations. Communication and transportation technology has started to plateau, which is a limiting feature for technology development. Like ancient empires, organizational political conflict and communication overhead will limit the ability of companies to improve technology and make AI smart

Using offline alternatives to tech

Often, it is helpful to use offline alternatives to tech, even if they mean taking additional time. Much of my research for this article came from physical books and newspapers. Conversations with other people were also very helpful for learning about issues related to civic advocacy.

For example, I went to the Charlie Clark Center for Local History at Arlington County's Center library to do research. I was looking for historical information about local advocacy in Arlington County.

During one visit there, I had a brief conversation with the manager about the research I was doing. I mentioned that I was looking for information about local advocay related to the planning of the DC Metro in the 1960s. Shortly after the conversation, he handed me a book called "The Great Society Subway by Zachary M. Schrag", which ended up being very helpful for my research due to the analysis of planning for the DC Metro.

Later, I ended up running into him during an event for the Arlington's Aurora Hills Library Centennial Celebration. We had a brief conversation about Arlington's support for school desegration, and he told me that Arlington was the second county in the nation to desegrate schools.

Looking through books and newspaper clippings took a significant amount of time. However, it was great for learning, and they weren't distracting me like the Internet would. There was also information that was not available online.

While offline activites are helpful, it is important to not completely avoid technology and ignore any instances where it is useful. I typed this article on a computer, and did not write it by hand on paper. Also, I looked through online newspaper archives to do research for this article.
