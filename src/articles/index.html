Urban design to support third places

Create Third Places also advocates for designing urban areas in a way that supports public spaces. Public spaces also promote civic engagement, which helps cities improve over time and mitigate challenges. Below are key issues that Create Third Places advocates for.

  • Improve access to community centers and libraries: Community centers and libraries are great examples of third places because they are free to use and open to everyone.
  • Reduced housing costs:

    Housing across the US is expensive, which prevents people from living in neighborhoods they would otherwise enjoy and forces people to move. Lower housing costs would help people continue living in places they enjoy for a long period of time. They develop a sense of familiarity that helps them work on improving neighborhoods.

    Higher housing prices will mean needing to pay staff more to live nearby. Staff of a third place should live nearby, and this helps them understand the neighborhood where the third place is located.

  • Support development by locals: People should be empowered to take a more active role in developing their own neighborhoods with small scale construction. Commercial and residential buildings constructed by locals are more likely to reflect what current and future residents want.
  • Support small businesses: Many great third places are run by small businesses. They are connected with the local community and develop an understanding of what people want. They are also a great way to provide jobs to people and make sure money stays in the local economy, which helps neighborhoods in the long term.
  • Travel acessibility: People should be able to easily travel to third places near where they live. This includes making areas around third places walkable and supporting a variety of transportation options.

Additional policy recommendations to support third places
  • Support unions: Unions will help people negoatiate for fair pay and benefits, in addition to less overtime. As a result, they will have more time and money to support third places.
  • Free health insurance for small business owners and employees: It is expensive for third place owners to pay for the costs of health insurance for themselves and their emnployeers. The cost of health insurance is also a better for people who want to create their own third place. If they did not have to pay for health insurance, they could spend more time focusing on supporting their third place over generating income. In the long term, a universal health care system should be implemented.
  • Expand hours for libraries and community centers.
