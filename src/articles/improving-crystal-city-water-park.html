Improving Crystal City Water Park

{{>authors/articleHeading name='Bharat Ponnaluri' date='July 2026' imageLink='https://createthirdplaces.nyc3.cdn.digitaloceanspaces.com/people/Bharat_Picture.jpg' }}

The Crystal City Water Park has the potential to be a great public gathering space. After a major renovation, it reopened in 2023 with food stalls and a large outdoor space with tables. The Water Park is within close walking distance of the Crystal City Metro station and more than 10,000 people live nearby. However, there are some challenges that are limiting it as a public gathering space.

Challenges with the Crystal City Water Park

In the winter of 2026, snow and ice combined with temperatures staying below freezing made the space unusable for more than a week. The snow and ice became snowcrete that was not possible to remove with a shovel. After the snow melted, the space became usable, although cold weather made the space uncomfortable.

These are representative of how the weather in Crystal City makes the Water Park impractical as a public space for much of the year. Often it is too cold or too hot. Rain and snow can also be an issue. Weather has also made it challenging for vendors to consistently get customers, and some permanently closed.

Empty seating area taken on evening in February 2026. (Photo by Bharat Ponnaluri) Vacant site of former ice cream vendor taken on evening in February 2026. (Photo by Bharat Ponnaluri)

Food also tends to be expensive. Buying a meal will typically cost significantly more than $10. Snack options are limited and expensive for the portion sizes.

Empty seating area taken on evening in February 2026. (Photo by Bharat Ponnaluri)

Ways to improve space

There are small steps that can be taken that make the Water Park significantly better as a public space. One quick step would be to have a vendor in one of the stalls sell low cost snacks and soft drinks for less than $5.

Adding an overhead roof over parts of the seating area and the main path would make the space more comfortable in the rain. A roof would also provide more shade during warm weather. Ceiling fans could also be added to provide additional cooling effect. More trees could also be planted near the seating area for more shade and a small cooling effect during the summer. Trees would also enhance the visual appeal of the space.
