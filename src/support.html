Other suggestions
  • Walk through a public park and saying hi to a stranger who looks towards you with a friendly smile.
  • Briefly talk with a stranger in a public space if they indicate an openness to conversation.
  • Show up to a public event being hosted at a community center.
  • Volunteer at your local public library.

Third places are created by people taking initiative to support third places. Think about something you can do this week. Actions do not have to be big and taking a few minutes per day helps. Also, taking time to show other people how great third places is helpful.

Additional resources
  • Strong Towns guide on building a third place through tactical urbanism. Strong Towns also post articles emphasizing the importance of bottom up driven change
  • Better Block is a nonprofit that helps communities create public spaces through a grassroots urban design approach. Better Block also maintains Wikiblock, an open source set of designs for items that can be added to public spaces.
  • Leading a Jane's walk: A Jane's Walk is a citizen-led walking conversation with the goals of sharing neighborhood stories, learning about one's local community, and connecting with neighbors. The concept of a Jane's walk was inspired by urbanist Jane Jacobs, who advocated for a community-based approach towards building cities.
