Recommended Books

By Bharat Ponnaluri

Here are some books related to promoting third places that I recommend

Books I have read

  • Strong Towns: A Bottom-up Revolution to Rebuild American Prosperity by Charles Marohn
    This book makes a compelling case for how bottom up driven community improvements are beneficial. On the website for his nonprofit Strong Towns, there are a variety of useful guides on how people can create third places.
    Marohn worked as a transportation engineer before starting Strong Towns, and wrote a book called Confessions of a Recovering Engineer. As an engineer who is working on software that prioritizes improving local communities, I personally relate.
  • The Lonely Century by Noreena Hertz:
    I found this book to a great resource for explaining how we can improve human connection, which was helpful in my attempts to promote third places through hosting events.

Here are some other books that I've read parts of.

  • The Great Good Place by Ray Oldenburg
    Ray Oldenburg contained the term "third place" in this book. Karen Christensen has also recently published a revised edition

  • The Death and Life of Great American Cities
  • The Art of Gathering by Priya Parker
