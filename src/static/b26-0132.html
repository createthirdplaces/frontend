Letter by Bharat Ponnaluri in support of B26-0132 - Expanding Community Access to Safe and Clean Recreational Space Act of 2025

B26-0132 is proposed legislation in DC that will expand community access to schools on evening and weekend hours for recreational purposes. Here is the full text of the bill

I am a resident of DC living in Dupont Circle. I support B26-0132 in order to expand access to community meeting space. I run a public community board game group that is looking for meeting space, and I’m a member of Strong Towns DMV.

Over the past 10 years, I have noticed a significant decline in the availability of accessible community gathering spaces in DC, and the government should step in. Back in 2015, many of the community gathering spaces were in bars and restaurants that are no longer options. Many places have closed, shortened their hours, or raised food prices. While many new restaurants and bars have opened up, the ones with space typically have high food prices that limit accessibility.

The hardest part of organizing community events is finding accessible space in regards to cost, opening hours, and room size. Other groups I’ve talked to have had similar challenges, including one I was a part of that moved it’s regular location 4 times within the span of a year, in addition to various temporary locations that didn’t work.

Two spaces I remember are the Panera on the southern side of DuPont Circle and the Noodles and Company on 17th Street near Farragut Square. They had a variety of low-cost food items so that the cost of food was not a major accessibility barrier. Unfortunately, both these places closed and turned into vacant commercial lots.

I think the legislation should be passed for the following benefits.
  • Incremental neighborhood improvements are best done by residents working together. More community spaces would give more opportunities for neighbors to connect with each other, and form friendships that will translate into working on neighborhood improvements.
  • Public health would improve as the community spaces include sports facilities where people can exercise.
  • Expanded access to local recreational spaces would make people more connected with their communities and neighbors. In DC, travelling to a local space would likely involve walking and possibly public transit. This allows them to get a better view of their community and connect with other locals. At these community spaces, people would form in person connections with their community.
